ID20012(A) Design Studio 1

Tag Fortune - Development Phase 2

A quick intervention prototype installed near the entrance to test whether a small NFC-triggered action can shift behavior at the moment of card tagging.

Prototype Snapshot

What We Observed

People naturally pause to tag their card before entering the building. Placing an NFC interaction at that exact moment lowered friction to participation. Most users tapped once, viewed the fortune quickly, and moved on without disrupting flow at the entrance.

Phase 2 Reflection

Did it work as intended?

Mostly yes. The intervention was understandable and fast enough to use in transit.

Is the idea still right, or did the prototype reveal something better?

The core idea still holds. The prototype suggests that placement and physical form matter as much as screen content.

What will change before the real execution?

Improve visual cues on the object, shorten interaction time, and refine messaging so first-time users understand the action instantly.

What This Webpage Adds

This page is not only a passive record. At the site, opening this URL gives visitors context (why the object exists), immediate interaction (fortune experience), and evidence of iteration (what changed and why). It extends the physical intervention into a documented, living part of the project.
